# Portfolio Website

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that showcases technical skills, projects, and professional identity through an immersive retro computing aesthetic. The portfolio itself IS the design statement — the CRT monitor interface is not decoration, it's the product experience.

## Users

- **Hiring managers & recruiters** — evaluating technical depth, taste, and personality
- **Other developers** — assessing skill level, design sensibility, and creative approach
- **Designer/Developer audience** — people who appreciate craft, attention to detail, and unconventional design choices

## Brand Voice & Tone

- **Nostalgic but contemporary** — evokes 1980s computing without irony; taken seriously as aesthetic choice
- **Technical & precise** — monospace typography, pixel-perfect execution, authentic CRT effects
- **Confident, not trying too hard** — the aesthetic choice speaks for itself; no explanation needed
- **Interactive & tactile** — hover effects, glitch animations, responsive feedback; the site feels alive

## Strategic Principles

1. **Aesthetic is the product** — every detail reinforces the retro computing world. Nothing breaks the fourth wall.
2. **Craft over template** — handcrafted CRT monitor bezel, 3D perspective, physics-based shadows; zero off-the-shelf aesthetics
3. **Meaningful interaction** — animations and effects have purpose (glitch on hover, green phosphor glow, scanlines) not just decoration
4. **Depth & layers** — pseudo-elements for screen effects, radial gradients for glow, multiple z-index layers for the monitor metaphor

## Anti-References

- Flat, minimal portfolios (generic, forgettable)
- Glass-morphism or translucency (trendy, shallow)
- Stock photography (inauthentic)
- Generic gradient backgrounds (lazy)
- Identical card grids (template reflex)

## Register

**Brand** — The portfolio itself is the design statement. This is identity-driven, not a tool serving another product.
